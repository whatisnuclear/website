NUCLEAR ENERGY?

Atoms have protons, neutrons, and electrons (derived from BruceBlaus)

To understand nuclear energy, you must know what a nucleus is. It's the dense central core of an atom that contains neutrons and protons.

(2012rc)

The number of protons in the nucleus determines which element an atom is. If you look at all possible proton numbers, you'll find the periodic table of the elements. For a chemist, this is all you need. But within each of these boxes, there may be over a dozen different types of atoms that all have the same number of protons, but different numbers of neutrons. Such things are called isotopes.

If you expand all the isotopes of each element you get something much larger than the table of the elements; you get the table of the nuclides. Nuclear engineers use this much more than the table of the elements.

Chart of the Nuclides zoomed out (National Nuclear Data Center)

Here's the full table of the nuclides. There are more than 4000 known nuclides.

Nuclei can undergo spontaneous or induced nuclear reactions, like these (CC-BY-SA LibreTexts)

Just like chemicals can go through chemical reactions, nuclei can go through nuclear reactions, where the nucleus is changed either spontaneously or due to an incident particle. Also like in chemistry, some nuclear reactions are exothermic and others are endothermic.

Nuclear fission: nucleus splits apart (MikeRun)

Nuclear reactors operate thanks to one particularly important nuclear reaction: nuclear fission. In this reaction, an incoming neutron enters the nucleus of a large atom (like uranium) and causes it to into two large chunks. This reaction is astoundingly exothermic, releasing a huge amount of energy.

Fission yields from fissioning U-235 (National Nuclear Data Center)

Here's the table of nuclides with the different fission products overlayed on it. This shows that you never know which two smaller atoms you'll get, and that you get a large variety of radioactive nuclides. These are the primary constituent of nuclear waste, which we'll talk more about soon.

Nuclear fusion: nuclei pushed together (src)

Nuclear fusion is an energetic nuclear reaction where energy is released when small atoms are combined (or fused). This is what powers the sun. We have successfully weaponized this energy, but have yet to get net energy out of it in peaceful applications (as of June 2023). Many people and companies are working to get fusion working for power today.
